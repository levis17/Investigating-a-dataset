Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

The TMDb movies dataset contains information on 10,000 movies collected from The Movies Database [TMDB], the information gathered from the database are categorized in twenty-one columns as follows;

  • id
  • imdb_id
  • popularity
  • budget
  • revenue
  • original_title
  • cast
  • homepage
  • director
  • tagline
  • keywords
  • overview
  • runtime
  • genres
  • production_companies
  • release_date
  • vote_count
  • vote_average
  • release_year
  • budget_adj
  • revenue_adj The last two columns represents the budget and revenue of the movies in terms of 2010 dollars.

Question(s) for Analysis¶

Some of the questions that can be considered are;

  • what makes a movie popular
  • what type of genres do viewers like

Conclusions¶

  • The runtime of a movie must be in the range of 150-200 minutes to make it become popular.
  • The popularity of a film does not depend on high budget as movies with lesser budget gained more popularity.
  • Same as the budget the lesser profitable movies gained more popularity.
  • Science fiction was the most popular genre among viewers.
  • Action movies gained more votes by viewers
  • Drama movies gained more votes by Average by the viewers

Limitations¶

  • After investigating it was discovered that the movies were 10,866 instead of 10,000, and along the line there were missing values,duplicates and so much '0' in the data.
  • There were entries in the rows separated with a delimiter '|' which resulted in picking the main entry from the rows which will eventually give a less accurate result.
